# Membrane Quick Reference: Wet Beads to Finished MEA

> Condensed checklist. For full details, safety notes, and theory, see the [complete membrane guide](./README.md).

## You Will Need

- Mixed bed DI resin (1-2 cups to start)
- Table salt + warm water + large bowl
- Fine mesh strainer or paint strainer bags
- Parchment paper + cookie sheet
- Dedicated spice grinder + dust mask + eye protection
- PVC cement (clear)
- Landscape fabric (non-woven PP)
- Flat surface + clamps/weights
- Multimeter (optional, for conductivity test)

## Steps

### 0. Rinse (10 min)
- Open bag, pour beads into mesh strainer.
- Rinse under tap water 30 seconds.
- Drain 5 minutes.

### 1. Separate (30-45 min)
- Make saturated brine in a tall container (salt until crystals stop dissolving).
- Pour rinsed beads into brine, stir, let settle 15-30 min.
- **Anion beads float, cation beads sink.**
- Scoop off anion beads (save for ED diode films or discard).
- Drain cation beads. Rinse to remove salt.

### 2a. Dry Cation Beads
- **Fast (2-4 hrs):** Spread on parchment-lined sheet, oven at 170-200°F, door cracked, stir every 30 min.
- **Slow (24-48 hrs):** Single layer on tray, fan optional.
- **Done when:** beads click together and shatter under spoon pressure.

### 2b. Grind (10-15 min)
- Coffee grinder or mortar and pestle.
- Grind to fine powder (powdered sugar consistency). Sieve if needed.
- Wear dust mask. Label container "GROUND SAC RESIN."

### 3. Mix Slurry (10-15 min)
- **50% ground SAC resin + 50% PVC cement by volume.** That's the whole recipe.
- Mix thoroughly — 2-3 minutes until uniform. No dry clumps, no clear spots.
- **NO carbon, NO graphite, NO charcoal** in the membrane mix. Electronic conductors = short circuit.
- Work quickly — 10-15 minutes of working time before PVC cement sets.

### 4. Cast Membrane (15 min + 24 hr cure)
- Cut landscape fabric 15-20mm larger than cell area on all sides.
- Spread slurry layer on flat surface. Press fabric into wet slurry.
- Spread more slurry on top. Total thickness ~1-2mm including fabric.
- Weight lightly. Cure 24 hours in a well-ventilated area.

### 5. Apply Electrode — Side 1 (15 min + 24 hr cure)
- See [electrode guide](../electrode/) for the full recipe.
- Mix Bowl A (Elmer's + cornstarch) into Bowl B (PVC cement + ground SAC resin).
- Paint onto one membrane face, ~1-2mm thick.
- Press soot-blackened stainless screen into wet layer.
- Dry completely (12-24 hours).
- Torch from the screen side.

### 6. Apply Electrode — Side 2 (15 min + 24 hr cure)
- Flip the cured assembly.
- Repeat Step 5 on the other face.

### 7. Embed Current Collector Screens
- Press screens into wet electrode surface before final cure.
- Cure 24 hours.

---

## Quality Checks (before installing in cell)

- [ ] **Light test:** hold up to a light — no pinholes or thin spots
- [ ] **Flex test:** bends without cracking (thick credit card flexibility)
- [ ] **Wet resistance test:** soak in water 10 min, multimeter reads < 50 kΩ across faces

---

## Done

The MEA is finished and ready for cell box assembly when:
- Both electrode layers are fully cured
- Current collector screens are embedded on both sides
- No pinholes, cracks, or delamination
