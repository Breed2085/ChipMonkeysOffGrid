# Electrolyte — Ferrous Chloride (FeCl₂)

## Quick Start (Muriatic Acid + Scrap Iron)

Buy muriatic acid (31% HCl) at Home Depot for ~$8/gallon. Dissolve scrap iron in it. Done in 24 hours.

### Process
1. Pour muriatic acid into an acid-safe container (HDPE, glass, or PP)
2. Add scrap mild steel pieces (nails, bolts, rebar, wire — magnet test: if it sticks, it's good)
3. It fizzes — hydrogen gas is being released. **DO THIS OUTDOORS. No flames.**
4. Wait until fizzing slows (12-24 hours)
5. Strain out undissolved iron
6. Result: ferrous chloride (FeCl₂) solution, pale green color, pH 1-2

### Iron Source Rules
| USE (magnet sticks) | AVOID |
|---------------------|-------|
| Rebar, nails, bolts | **Stainless steel** — chromium is carcinogenic, poisons membrane |
| Wire, angle iron | **Galvanized** — zinc contamination |
| Brake rotors, bed frames | **Tool steel** — tungsten, vanadium, cobalt contamination |
| Cast iron, engine blocks | **Plated metals** — nickel, chrome |

### Target Concentration
- ~1M FeCl₂ in dilute HCl
- pH 1-2
- Pale green to green color

## Advanced: SEM Cell (Make Your Own Acid From Salt)

See [sem-cell/](../sem-cell/) for the salt electro mining process that produces HCl from table salt and electricity.

### Lye Byproduct
The SEM process also produces sodium hydroxide (NaOH/lye) at the cathode. A 110-gallon batch produces ~84 lbs of NaOH worth $170-330 retail. Uses: soap making, drain cleaner, biodiesel, paint stripping. **The byproduct pays for the whole operation.**

## Cost Per Gallon (1M solution)
- Scrap iron: ~0.5 lb (free)
- Muriatic acid: ~$2 (from $8/gal jug)
- Or via SEM: ~$0.15 (table salt + electricity)

## Safety
- pH 1-2 acid — wear gloves and eye protection
- Hydrogen gas released during iron dissolution — ventilate, no flames
- Baking soda nearby for spill neutralization
- Skin contact: rinse immediately with water
